IN THE SUPERIOR COURT OF NEW YORK

Case Title: Adams Trading LLC v. Northline Logistics Inc.
Date: 2024-11-19

Summary of Facts:
Adams Trading LLC entered into a supply contract with Northline Logistics Inc.
under which Northline agreed to deliver industrial materials in three monthly
shipments. The contract included a penalty clause for late or incomplete
delivery and referenced Section 12 for notice and cure obligations.

Adams alleges that Northline failed to deliver the second shipment on time and
delivered only 40 percent of the third shipment. Adams sent written notice and
requested cure within ten business days. Northline did not fully cure and
argues that regional transport disruptions made performance impossible.

Claims:
1) Material breach of contract
2) Damages for lost business opportunities
3) Enforcement of contractual penalty clause

Defenses:
1) Commercial impracticability due to transport disruption
2) Partial performance substantially satisfied the contract
3) Penalty clause is unenforceable as punitive

Relief Requested:
Adams requests compensatory damages, contractual penalties, and attorney fees.
